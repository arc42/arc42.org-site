Software architects reuse a handful of words so often that their meaning quietly
erodes. In *What's in a Name?* Gernot Starke takes one loaded term at a time and asks
what it should actually mean, why the vague version causes trouble, and how a sharper
definition helps teams communicate.

## The series

- **[Architecture](https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/whats-in-a-name-architecture/)**
  — what "software architecture" really denotes, grounded in the IEEE 1471 definition.
- **[Quality](https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/2021/03/whats-in-a-name-qualitaet/)**
  — why "quality" means something different to every stakeholder, and how to make it concrete.
- **[Evaluate](https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/whats-in-a-name-evaluate/)**
  — the difference between evaluating business impact and merely analysing problems.
- **[Transparency](https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/whats-in-a-name-transparency/)**
  — how frameworks that hide complexity make systems less transparent, not more.
- **[Consistency](https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/whats-in-a-name-consistency/)**
  — the many senses of "consistency", from UIs to databases, and why conceptual integrity matters.
