What is arc42?

arc42 answers two questions in a pragmatic way: what should you document and communicate about your architecture, and how. It grew out of practical experience with many systems across domains, from web and information systems to real-time, embedded and data-intensive systems.

  • Supports arbitrary technologies and tools, nothing to install.
  • Process-agnostic, and especially well suited to lean and agile teams.
  • Available in 12 languages and every common format: AsciiDoc, Markdown, Word, LaTeX and more.
  • Open source and free of charge, in commercial and private settings alike.

Made by two people who still do the work

We built arc42 so you spend your time on architecture, not on formatting. Dr. Peter Hruschka and Dr. Gernot Starke started the template in 2005, and after more than 20 years it is still shaped by what actually happens in projects: workshops, reviews, code, and the occasional heated whiteboard discussion.

That is also why arc42 stays free. Good architecture communication should not depend on a license budget.
